1. The apex limit for a personal loan sanction is set at Rs. 10,00,000.
2. This maximum loan amount of Ten Lakh Rupees is strictly contingent on the applicant's eligibility assessment.
3. Applicants must demonstrate a minimum requisite credit score of 650 to qualify.
4. A credit score below the threshold of 650 will lead to an immediate rejection of the application.
5. The FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) must not exceed a maximum cap of 50%.
6. If the calculated FOIR surpasses the 50% limit, the loan cannot be approved under current guidelines.
7. The longest permissible repayment period (maximum tenure) is fixed at 60 months.
8. This 60-month tenure translates directly to a maximum repayment term of five years.
9. Loan amounts requested over Rs. 10,00,000 will be automatically truncated or rejected.
10. Eligibility criteria encompass multiple factors beyond just credit score and FOIR.
11. The minimum qualifying credit score of 650 is non-negotiable for all applicants.
12. FOIR is calculated based on existing EMI commitments and the proposed loan's EMI.
13. Shorter loan tenures, such as 12, 24, 36, or 48 months, are also available options.
14. The policy is designed to mitigate risk by setting a stringent maximum loan amount.
15. A high credit score (e.g., 750 or above) may increase the likelihood of receiving the maximum amount.
16. The final sanctioned loan amount is always 'subject to eligibility' and internal risk assessment.
17. Repayment structure must ensure the entire loan is paid off within the 60-month window.
18. Any deviation from the maximum 50% FOIR requires specific, high-level management approval (rarely granted).
19. Interest rates will be determined by the credit score, with 650 being the minimum entry point.
20. The current policy mandates adherence to these four primary constraints for personal loans.
21. Loans cannot be offered for a tenure of 72 months or any period exceeding 60 months.
22. Applicants should verify their current credit bureau score prior to formal application.
23. Rs. 10,00,000 is an absolute upper limit, even for applicants with impeccable credit.
24. FOIR calculation includes all existing monthly fixed obligations and proposed loan EMI.
25. Minimum loan amount is not explicitly stated but is implicitly lower than the maximum.
26. Policy review for these parameters is typically conducted on a quarterly basis.
27. The required 650 credit score ensures a base level of borrower repayment reliability.
28. Strict adherence to the 50% FOIR limit prevents over-leveraging of the borrower's income.
29. The maximum tenure of 60 months keeps the overall loan exposure duration controlled.
